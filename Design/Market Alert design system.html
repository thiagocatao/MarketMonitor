$ Market Alert Design System · v1.0

A quiet watch over the markets.

Market Alert lives in your menu bar. Most days it says nothing. When something breaks, it speaks in two colors — green and red — and gets out of your way. This document is the visual grammar that keeps it calm.

01 — Brand

Restraint, with one alarm.

The product earns trust by saying nothing most of the time. Type is plain, surfaces are paper-white, color is monochrome — until a trigger fires and the interface becomes loud, briefly, in red. Then it returns to silence.

02 — Color

Black, white, and only when needed — green and red.

The palette has four working roles: ink for type and primary surfaces, paper for everything light, up / green for gains and confirmations, down / red reserved for losses, alerts, and the status item itself. No blues, no oranges, no gradients besides one soft red wash on the alert banner.

Ink

Type, controls, the active state of the filter chip and primary button. Never neutral gray — slightly cool true-black.

Rules of engagement
Red is rare.The menu bar icon, the pulse, the alert banner, and rows that breached a threshold. Nothing else.
Green is data, not decoration.Only on numbers — never as a button background or a brand surface.
No gradients.One exception: a vertical wash on the alert banner, ≤6% saturation, top-to-bottom.
03 — Typography

Geist, with one indulgence.

Geist (sans) carries the entire product UI. Geist Mono carries every number — every ticker, price, percent, and timestamp — with tabular figures so vertical columns of digits never jitter. Instrument Serif is reserved for editorial moments: marketing, the design-system cover, an onboarding empty state. It is never used inside the popover.

04 — Space & Shape

A four-pixel rhythm.

Everything is on a 4 px grid. The popover lives on its tightest scale because every row competes for attention with the next; marketing surfaces breathe at twice the density. Radii are small, but never sharp.

05 — Components

Eight pieces, well-mannered.

The popover is built from a handful of small components reused at scale. Every component has at most two states beyond default: enabled / disabled for toggles, up / down / strong for change pills, clear / alert for status. There are no skins.

06 — Icons

Hairline, no fill.

Lucide at 1.5–1.6 stroke, rounded joins. Always rendered in ink — never green or red. The only colored glyphs in the entire product are the $ menu bar mark, the triangular alert glyph in the banner, and the − sign on a strong-down pill.
